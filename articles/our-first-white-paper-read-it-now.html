A Review of our Phenomenal Inaugural Event!

"The Lived Experiences of BCI Users" is a transcript from our inaugural event back in October 2025, featuring a panel discussion with three members of the BCI Pioneers Coalition — Ian Burkhart, Dr. J. Galen Buckwalter, and Scott Imbrie — all of whom have lived with spinal cord injuries and participated in invasive BCI research using implanted electrode arrays.

Key Themes

The panellists unanimously described participation as profoundly meaningful, with the psychological and social benefits — sense of purpose, community, and belonging to a research team — outweighing any direct physical gains.

Galen made a passionate case for creative applications like his neural music project, arguing that the field neglects quality of life by focusing almost exclusively on motor restoration. Ian pointed to a tension between academic incentives (novelty, publication) and the translational work needed to get devices into people's hands. Scott's "Texas Instruments calculator" analogy captured the group's philosophy well: release simple, functional tools and let them grow, rather than waiting for perfection.

The panellists highlighted that participation requires significant caregiver support, financial resources, and geographic proximity to research centres — factors that exclude many who might benefit. Consent processes that emphasise risks over benefits were also noted to deter caregivers unnecessarily.

Success, from their perspective, means equitable access, genuine user autonomy, flexibility for creative uses, and meaningful quality of life improvements — not just technical performance metrics.
